        <section id="intro">
            <h2>What is it?</h2>
            <p>The Equivalence Principle states that the effects of <strong>gravity</strong> and the effects of <strong>acceleration</strong> are locally indistinguishable. If you were in a closed box in deep space being pushed upward, you wouldn't be able to tell if you were moving or just sitting on Earth.</p>
        </section>

        <section id="elevator">
            <h2>The Famous Thought Experiment</h2>
            <p>Imagine you are inside an elevator with no windows:</p>
            <div class="comparison-grid">
                <div class="box">
                    <h3>Scenario A: Earth</h3>
                    <p>The elevator is sitting on the ground. You feel your weight because gravity pulls you down.</p>
                </div>
                <div class="box">
                    <h3>Scenario B: Deep Space</h3>
                    <p>A rocket accelerates the elevator at 9.8 m/s². You feel the exact same "weight" pressing you to the floor.</p>
                </div>
            </div>
            <p><strong>Result:</strong> Any experiment you perform (dropping a ball, weighing yourself) will yield the same result in both scenarios!</p>
        </section>

        <section id="math">
            <h2>The Physics Behind It</h2>
            <p>In classical mechanics, we distinguish between two types of mass:</p>
            <ul>
                <li><strong>Inertial Mass:</strong> Resistance to acceleration (F = ma).</li>
                <li><strong>Gravitational Mass:</strong> How much gravity pulls on an object (F = mg).</li>
            </ul>
            <p>Einstein realized these are <em>exactly the same</em>. This led to the conclusion that gravity isn't just a force, but a curvature of spacetime.</p>
